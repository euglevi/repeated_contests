• The prize in each stage is worth 100 tokens.
  • The exact chance of winning the prize in each stage is given by the number of lottery tickets bought by you, divided by the total number of lottery tickets bought by both you and your co-participant.
  • More precisely, if you buy X lottery tickets and your co-participant buys Y lottery tickets, then your chance of winning the prize is X/(X+Y), and your co-participant’s chance of winning is Y/(X+Y).
  • For any number of lottery tickets purchased by your co-participant, the more lottery tickets you buy, the higher your chance of winning the lottery; the fewer lottery tickets you buy, the lower your chances of winning the lottery. The same applies for your co-participant.
Example

  • If you purchase 10 lottery tickets and your co-participant purchases 10 lottery tickets, then the total number of lottery tickets is 20.
  • Your chance of winning is 10/20 = 1/2 = 50%.
  • Your co-participant’s chance of winning is 10/20 = 1/2 = 50%.

  • Once both you and your co-participant have chosen how many lottery tickets to buy, the computer will determine which participant wins the prize by randomly selecting one of the tickets.
  • If one of your tickets is chosen, you win the prize.
  • If one of your co-participant’s tickets is chosen, they win the prize.
